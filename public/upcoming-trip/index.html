5

Contingency Notes

☀

Extreme heat (95–100°F+)

Front-load anything strenuous before 11am–noon, then retreat to the Desert Pearl Inn pool or AC through the hottest hours — both day cards above are built around this. Carry more water than feels necessary, and watch the kids for early heat-exhaustion signs: fatigue, headache, nausea, or a stop in sweating. If anyone shows those signs, get into shade/AC and hydrate immediately rather than pushing on. The Zion Human History Museum (air conditioned) is a good bail-out stop on the hottest afternoons.

⛈

Flash flood risk in the Narrows

Even kid-depth, bottom-up wading is subject to closures and warnings. Check the daily flash-flood forecast posted at the Zion Canyon Visitor Center (or the NPS Zion app) the morning of each Narrows attempt. If the forecast reads "possible" or worse, or there's any thunderstorm activity in the region — even miles upstream and out of sight — skip the river that day. Swap in the paved portion of Riverside Walk, Emerald Pools, or the Day 3 flex slot instead. Never enter the narrow canyon sections during or right after a storm, even if it looks clear locally.

⚑

Angels Landing lottery

If an adult wants to attempt it solo, apply to the seasonal lottery (opens roughly two months ahead) or try the day-before lottery via recreation.gov. Given the chains and exposed drop-offs, it's not recommended as a family hike with elementary-age kids — the itinerary above swaps in Canyon Overlook Trail on Day 3, which delivers similar views without the exposure.

↻

Built-in flex day

Day 3 (Monday) is intentionally unscheduled. If heat, a flood warning, or just tired kids knock out a Day 2 plan, move it to Day 3 rather than trying to force everything into one day.
